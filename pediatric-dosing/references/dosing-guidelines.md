# Pediatric Dosing Reference

Quick reference for pediatric weight-based medication dosing calculations.

## Weight-Based Dosing Formula

```
Dose (mg) = Weight (kg) ร Dose (mg/kg)
```

For liquid formulations:
```
Volume (mL) = Dose (mg) รท Concentration (mg/mL)
```

## Pediatric Formulation Selection

| Formulation | Typical Age Range | Notes |
|-------------|------------------|-------|
| Drops | 0-12 months | Concentrated, small volumes |
| Oral Suspension | 6 months - 6 years | Most common pediatric form |
| Chewable Tablets | 2-12 years | Child must be able to chew |
| Orally Disintegrating | 6+ years | Dissolves on tongue |
| Standard Tablets/Capsules | 8+ years | Must be able to swallow whole |

## Common Pediatric Medications - Quick Dosing

### Antibiotics

**Amoxicillin**
- Standard: 25-50 mg/kg/day divided q8-12h
- High-dose (AOM, sinusitis): 80-90 mg/kg/day divided q12h
- Max single dose: 500 mg (standard), 1000 mg (high-dose)

**Azithromycin**
- 10 mg/kg Day 1, then 5 mg/kg Days 2-5
- Max: 500 mg Day 1, 250 mg Days 2-5

**Cephalexin**
- 25-50 mg/kg/day divided q6-12h
- Max: 4 g/day

### Analgesics/Antipyretics

**Acetaminophen**
- 10-15 mg/kg/dose q4-6h
- Max: 75 mg/kg/day or 4 g/day (whichever is less)

**Ibuprofen**
- 5-10 mg/kg/dose q6-8h
- Max: 40 mg/kg/day or 2.4 g/day

### Antihistamines

**Diphenhydramine**
- 1-1.25 mg/kg/dose q6h
- Max: 300 mg/day

**Cetirizine**
- 6 months-2 years: 2.5 mg daily
- 2-5 years: 2.5-5 mg daily
- 6+ years: 5-10 mg daily

### Respiratory

**Albuterol (nebulized)**
- 0.15 mg/kg/dose (min 2.5 mg, max 5 mg)
- q20min x3 for acute asthma

**Prednisolone**
- Acute asthma: 1-2 mg/kg/day (max 60 mg)
- Duration: 3-5 days typically

## Safety Reminders

1. **Always verify** calculated doses against published max doses
2. **Double-check** decimal placement for concentrations
3. **Use actual weight** - avoid estimation when possible
4. **Consider renal/hepatic function** for dose adjustments
5. **Age-appropriate formulation** - verify child can take chosen form
